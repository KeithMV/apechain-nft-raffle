# Your First 5 White-Label Outreach Emails - Step by Step

## Step 1: Find Your First 5 Prospects (30 minutes)

### Target Profile: Small-Medium NFT Projects
- **5,000-50,000 holders** (big enough to afford $8K, small enough to respond)
- **Active Discord/Twitter** (shows they care about community engagement)
- **Recent activity** (launching, growing, engaging community)

### Where to Find Them:

#### Option A: Twitter Search (Easiest)
1. Go to Twitter.com
2. Search: "NFT project" OR "NFT collection" OR "NFT community"
3. Filter by "Latest" tweets
4. Look for projects with 1K-10K followers
5. Check their Discord/website for contact info

#### Option B: OpenSea Browse
1. Go to OpenSea.io
2. Browse collections with 1K-10K items
3. Look for active projects (recent sales, social links)
4. Find their Twitter/Discord from collection page

#### Option C: Discord Servers
1. Join popular NFT Discord servers
2. Look in #partnerships or #collaborations channels
3. Find projects looking for tools/services
4. Note their contact info

### Your First 5 Targets (Examples):
1. **Local crypto meetup organizer** (easiest to reach)
2. **New NFT project on ApeChain** (perfect fit)
3. **Gaming guild with NFTs** (active community)
4. **Small established collection** (proven demand)
5. **Crypto influencer with community** (personal brand)

## Step 2: Find Contact Information (15 minutes per prospect)

### Where to Look:
- **Twitter bio** (often has email or Discord)
- **Website contact page** (business email)
- **Discord server** (DM the founder/admin)
- **LinkedIn** (search founder name + project)
- **Linktree/bio links** (contact info)

### What You're Looking For:
- **Founder/CEO email** (best)
- **Business development email** (good)
- **General contact email** (okay)
- **Discord username** (backup)

## What to Expect:

### Realistic Response Rates:
- **50% will ignore** (normal)
- **30% will say no** (also normal)  
- **15% will ask questions** (good!)
- **5% will want a demo** (success!)

### Success Metrics:
- **1 response = good**
- **1 demo call = great**
- **1 interested prospect = amazing**

## Common Responses & How to Handle:

### "We're not interested"
**Response**: "No problem! If that changes, here's the demo: apechain-raffles.com"

### "What's your pricing?"
**Response**: "$8K setup + $850/month. Includes full branding, deployment, and ongoing support. When would be a good time for a quick demo?"

### "Can you send more info?"
**Response**: "Absolutely! Here's the live platform: apechain-raffles.com. I can show you exactly how it would look with your branding. Are you free for a 15-minute call this week?"

### "We don't have budget"
**Response**: "I understand. For context, this typically pays for itself with 15-20 raffles per month through platform fees. Would it help to see the revenue potential in a quick demo?"

## Remember:
- **Rejection is normal** - even successful salespeople get rejected 80% of the time
- **Personalization matters** - mention something specific about their project
- **Keep it short** - busy people don't read long emails
- **Follow up** - most deals happen after 3+ touchpoints
- **Be helpful** - focus on their community engagement, not your product
